The Editor\
*Economics Letters*

Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit "Algorithmic Pricing in a Many-Seller Market: Evidence from Airbnb" for consideration at *Economics Letters*.

The paper provides the first quasi-experimental evidence on the market-level effects of algorithmic pricing in a many-seller differentiated-product platform market. Using a sharp regression discontinuity design exploiting Airbnb's September 2023 pricing tool rollout across eight U.S. cities and over 22 million listing-day observations, I find that price effects are positive in all eight cities (sign test $p = 0.004$) but imprecisely estimated in magnitude under city-level wild cluster bootstrap ($p = 0.15$--$0.20$). The effects are substantially smaller than the 38% margin increases documented in oligopolistic markets by Assad et al. (2024, *JPE*), suggesting that market structure mediates the competitive effects of algorithmic pricing.

This paper is timely given the DOJ v. RealPage litigation (settled November 2025), the FTC's ongoing surveillance pricing investigation, and growing regulatory interest in algorithmic competition. The focused finding---that algorithmic pricing effects in many-seller markets are far smaller than in oligopolies---offers a clear, policy-relevant contribution well-suited to *Economics Letters*.

The manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere. A longer working paper exploring additional dimensions (price variance, heterogeneity analysis, detailed policy discussion) is in preparation for separate submission to a field journal.

Thank you for your consideration.
