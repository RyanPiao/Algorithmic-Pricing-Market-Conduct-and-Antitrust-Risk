The Editor\
*Journal of Urban Economics*

Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit "Algorithmic Pricing and Market Conduct: Evidence from Airbnb Smart Pricing" for consideration at the *Journal of Urban Economics*.

This paper studies how platform-provided pricing algorithms affect short-term rental markets across eight major U.S. cities. Using a sharp regression discontinuity design exploiting Airbnb's September 2023 pricing tool rollout and over 22 million listing-day observations, I find that price effects are positive in all eight cities (sign test $p = 0.004$) but imprecisely estimated in magnitude under city-level wild cluster bootstrap ($p = 0.15$--$0.20$). The effects are bounded between $-0.5\%$ and $+1.9\%$ --- substantially below the 38% found in oligopolistic markets. Importantly, city-level heterogeneity tracks regulatory stringency: larger effects in less-regulated markets (Chicago, Seattle) and smaller effects in heavily regulated markets (Los Angeles, San Francisco), suggesting that existing STR regulations may already constrain the algorithm's pricing impact.

I believe this paper is well-suited for the *Journal of Urban Economics* for three reasons. First, it directly addresses how platform technologies reshape urban housing and rental markets. Second, the eight-city design leverages cross-city variation in market structure and regulation --- a feature that JUE readers will find informative. Third, the policy implications speak directly to city-level STR regulation debates, complementing the federal antitrust perspective that has dominated the algorithmic pricing literature.

A shorter version of this paper, focused solely on the price-level finding without the city heterogeneity analysis, variance results, or policy discussion, is currently under review at *Economics Letters*. The present manuscript is substantially longer and broader in scope.

Thank you for your consideration.
